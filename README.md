## Instructional videos

I have a video/film degree and have previously worked as a commercial video producer. Here are a few instructional video projects I've worked on:

* [Get to know the New Relic platform](https://docs.newrelic.com/docs/new-relic-solutions/new-relic-one/introduction-new-relic-platform). This was a video I made towards the end of my 7.5 years at New Relic, aiming to give new and potential customers a quick, useful overview of the platform. Making videos about how the UI works can be a challenge, due to the simple fact that things often change; this means that it can be hard to justify spending much time on the videos, knowing they will likely need to be redone again soon, and that in turn means that often the videos just don't get made. This video was an attempt to show that us tech writers could fairly quickly produce a video about the UI, and that it didn't need to be perfect or highly polished to be highly helpful to customers. I intentionally scripted and recorded this video quite quickly (the whole production process took less than a day) to show to our team that this could be done. If we had a test case of how easy it could be, then we wouldn't be so worried about remaking them in future, and it would encourage us to do more. This video is still up in a prominent, high-traffic doc on their site 3 years after I made it, which I think attests to the quality and my skills in making it somewhat future-proofed. 
* [A video examining a deceptive fraud in the behavior/psychology space](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gsOmOonPTl0). For my podcast, I have sometimes done more in-depth video productions. I include this one to showcase some of my video production/editing skills.
